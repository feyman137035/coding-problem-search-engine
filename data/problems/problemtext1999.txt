Given a positive integern, find thepivot integerxsuch that:The sum of all elements between1andxinclusively equals the sum of all elements betweenxandninclusively.Returnthe pivot integerx. If no such integer exists, return-1. It is guaranteed that there will be at most one pivot index for the given input.Example 1:Input:n = 8Output:6Explanation:6 is the pivot integer since: 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 6 = 6 + 7 + 8 = 21.Example 2:Input:n = 1Output:1Explanation:1 is the pivot integer since: 1 = 1.Example 3:Input:n = 4Output:-1Explanation:It can be proved that no such integer exist.Constraints:1 <= n <= 1000
